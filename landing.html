The idea

A log of your day.
Kept honestly.

Every night, you talk. It writes it down — then tells you what it actually heard. Not a diary. A record with an editor who won't let things slide.

The record

One session. Three questions. Five minutes, before bed.

Every night it asks you three things. The first one holds you to what you said yesterday. The other two are new. That's the whole ritual.

Q1 — FOLLOWS UP ON LAST NIGHTMAR 14, 11:41 PM

"Yesterday you said you'd hit the gym before the deck. Did that happen?"
— "...no. Deck ran long, told myself I'd go tomorrow instead."

Q2 — NEWMAR 14, 11:43 PM

"What's one thing today that actually went the way you wanted?"
— "...honestly, the client call. Went better than I expected."

Q3 — NEWMAR 14, 11:45 PM

"What are you telling yourself about tomorrow, right now?"
— "...that tomorrow's the real start. Like today wasn't."

Combined Debrief MAR 14 — TONIGHT'S SESSION

This is the second night in a row the gym plan moved to "tomorrow." Notice the pattern: you're good at showing up for other people — the call went well because someone else was waiting on you. The gym has no one waiting. That's worth sitting with before you make tomorrow's promise.

Marginalia — how it works
i.

Talk, don't type

Open it before bed and say what happened. No blank page. No prompts to overthink.

ii.

It writes the entry

Your rambling becomes a clean, dated record automatically — the log you'd never bother keeping by hand.

iii.

It reads between the lines

Across entries, it catches what repeats — and says so, in the margin, in plain language. No charts. Just the truth.
