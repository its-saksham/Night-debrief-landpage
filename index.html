The idea

Say what you'll do.
Get held to it.

Every night, it asks if you did the thing you said you'd do yesterday. Then it listens to your day. Five minutes of talking becomes a record that actually remembers.

The record

One session. Three questions. Five minutes, before bed.

The first question checks in on what you said yesterday — no verdict, just a straight answer either way. The other two are new. That's the whole ritual.

Marginalia — how it works
i.

Talk, don't type

Open it before bed and say what happened. No blank page. No prompts to overthink.

ii.

It remembers so you don't have to

A notebook doesn't ask what happened to yesterday's plan. This does — every night, automatically, without you re-reading old pages.

iii.

It reads between the lines

Across entries, it catches what repeats — and says so, in plain language. No charts. Just the truth.

iv.

Yours, and only yours

What you say stays in your log. Not sold, not shared, not used to train anything beyond your own record.

v.

No streaks to break

Miss a night, skip a plan — it's just noted, not flagged. There's no score resetting to zero. Tomorrow starts clean either way.
